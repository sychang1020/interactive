A HOME IN GRAVEYARD FIELDS
for Maverick

                                                                                    1.
                                                                                  Follow the stream
                                                                           to the swimming hole,
                                                                    its bubbling specks
                                                              the color of fish scales,
                                                     like oil stains
                                          pooling and grooving.
                             Spot the wet
                      picnic tables
             outside the pizza shack,
Nantahala river rafters
caught in yesterday's rainstorm.
The wooded island floats
above lake water.
  No hands on the wheel.
    I run
        ahead of you
          duck-diving under kudzu,
            we paddle out at daybreak—
            cold beers in koozies.

          2.
           Woke before dawn
             somewhere in Tennessee,                                                                                           4.
               still dark in a quiet campground.                                                                          Rainwater glides down
             You, asleep next to me,                                                                                backcountry trails
         all the blankets                                                                               green with baby clover
         bunched at your feet.                                                                and pools of algae,

3.                                                                                                    the soft pillow tops of mushrooms
We look for the next sleep,                                                                        scuffed with mud.
a place to play house.                                                                                     We zip our sleeping bags together,

                  We find it                                                                                               crickets on the rainfly.
                    Deep in the Black Hills.                                                                                    I have never felt closer to you than I did
                      Our first homemade meal, a friend                                                                            when the entire world fit inside
                        offers her childhood bed.                                                                                    that home in the mountains.
                          Small town Montana. Quick stop for
                            gas station ice cream
                              on the back of four-wheelers.
                                In the weed farmer's shipping container,

                              a hint of patchouli and years of dog piss,
                            with a slotted view of semis on the highway,
                          above it the last stretch
                        of eastern Sierras.
                      Canyon country. Swimming
                    through sunlit
                  bends, packs above our heads,
                  the cairns guide us to
                    a hidden grove.
                      Over the hump of Dixie,
                    a town that sounds like Penguin,
                  motel shower sex after a sixteen-mile hike.
                  I don't remember falling asleep.
